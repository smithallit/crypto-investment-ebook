## Chapter 1: What Is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital asset that uses cryptography and decentralized networks to transfer value.

### Core Ideas
- Digital scarcity  
- Peer-to-peer transfer  
- Open, global networks  

### Why It Matters for Investors
Crypto introduces a new asset class with unique risk/reward characteristics.

### Key Takeaway
Before investing, understand what gives an asset value and why people use it.

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 2: Blockchain Fundamentals

A blockchain is a distributed ledger maintained by many participants.

### Concepts to Know
- Blocks and transactions  
- Consensus mechanisms (PoW, PoS)  
- Decentralization tradeoffs  

### Investor Lens
Technology quality can affect security, scalability, and long-term adoption.

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 3: Risk Management First

Risk management is more important than picking the "perfect" coin.

### Rules
- Never invest money you cannot afford to lose  
- Set maximum position sizes  
- Diversify appropriately  
- Use a written plan  

### Emotional Discipline
Avoid fear-driven buying and panic selling.

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 4: Wallets, Exchanges, and Security

Security mistakes are one of the biggest causes of loss.

### Security Basics
- Use strong, unique passwords  
- Enable 2FA  
- Protect seed phrases offline  
- Prefer hardware wallets for long-term storage  

### Exchange Safety
Use reputable exchanges and withdraw large long-term holdings to self-custody.

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 5: How to Evaluate Crypto Projects

Evaluate projects like a skeptical analyst.

### Checklist
- Problem being solved  
- Team and execution history  
- Token utility and distribution  
- Community and ecosystem health  
- On-chain activity and developer momentum  

### Red Flags
- Guaranteed return promises  
- Anonymous teams with no track record  
- Unsustainable token incentives  

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 6: Building an Investment Strategy

Choose a strategy matched to your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### Common Approaches
- Dollar-cost averaging (DCA)  
- Core-satellite portfolio  
- Long-term hold with periodic review  

### Create Rules
Define entry, sizing, review frequency, and exit criteria.

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 7: Portfolio Construction and Rebalancing

A portfolio should reflect conviction and risk limits.

### Example Buckets
- Large-cap crypto assets  
- Mid-cap growth assets  
- Small speculative allocation  
- Cash/stable reserve  

### Rebalancing
Rebalance periodically to maintain target allocations.

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 8: Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

### Frequent Mistakes
- Overconcentration  
- Chasing hype cycles  
- Ignoring fees and slippage  
- Neglecting security  
- Copying influencers blindly  

### Better Habits
Use checklists, journals, and predefined rules.

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 9: Taxes, Regulation, and Recordkeeping

Crypto tax treatment varies by jurisdiction and may change over time.

### Best Practices
- Keep detailed transaction records  
- Track cost basis  
- Save exchange statements  
- Consult a qualified tax professional  

### Compliance Mindset
Treat recordkeeping as part of your investment process.

<div class="page-break"></div>

## Chapter 10: Long-Term Learning Plan

Great investors keep learning.

### 90-Day Plan
- Week 1–2: Learn basics and security setup  
- Week 3–6: Build watchlist and evaluation process  
- Week 7–10: Start small with strict risk limits  
- Week 11–13: Review and refine strategy  

### Final Advice
Consistency and risk control beat excitement and prediction.
